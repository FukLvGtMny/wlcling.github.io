ʿārak עָרַךְ ‘(a) to arrange (b) to value’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: a primitive root | In another language: ערך | Variants: עָרַךְ, עָרַךְ | Morphology: (a) H:V (b) H:V

(a) 1) to arrange, set or put or lay in order, set in array, prepare, order, ordain, handle, furnish, esteem, equal, direct, compare
1a) (Qal)
1a1) to arrange or set or lay in order, arrange, state in order, set forth (a legal case), set in place
1a2) to compare, be comparable (b) 1) (Hiphil) to value, tax
