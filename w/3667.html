kǝnaʿan כְּנַ֫עַן ‘(a) Canaan (b) merchant’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: from H3665 | Roots: כנע | In another language: כנע | Variants: כְּנַ֫עַן, כְּנַ֫עַן | Morphology: (a) N:N-M-P + N:N--L + N:N--LG (b) N:N-M-P + N:N--L + N:N--LG

(a) Canaan = "lowland"
n pr m
1) the 4th son of Ham and the progenitor of the Phoenicians and of the various nations who peopled the seacoast of Palestine
n pr loc
2) the land west of the Jordan peopled by the descendants of Canaan and subsequently conquered by the Israelites under Joshua (b) 1) merchant, trader
