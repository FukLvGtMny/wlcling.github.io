yaʿar יַ֫עַר ‘(a) wood (b) honeycomb’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: from an unused root probably meaning to thicken with verdure | Variants: יַ֫עַר, יַ֫עַר | Morphology: (a) H:N-M (b) H:N-M

(a) 1) forest, wood, thicket, wooded height (b) 1) honeycomb
