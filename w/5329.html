nāṣaḥ נָצַח ‘to conduct’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: a primitive root | Morphology: V

nāṣaḥ1) to excel, be bright, be preeminent, be perpetual, be overseer, be enduring
1a) (Niphal) enduring (participle)
1b) (Piel) to act as overseer or superintendent or director or chief
