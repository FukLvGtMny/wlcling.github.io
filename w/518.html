ʾim אִם ‘(a) if (b) that if’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: a primitive particle | Variants: אִם, כִּי אם־ | Morphology: (a) H:PRT (b) H:PRT

(a) 1) if
1a) conditional clauses
1a1) of possible situations
1a2) of impossible situations
1b) oath contexts
1b1) no, not
1c) if...if, whether...or, whether...or...or
1d) when, whenever
1e) since
1f) interrogative particle
1g) but rather (b) 1) each particle retaining its independent force, and relating to a differentclause
1a) that if
