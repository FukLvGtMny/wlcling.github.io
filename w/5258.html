nāsak נָסַךְ ‘1to pour 2to install’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: a primitive root | In another language: נסך | Variants: נָסַךְ, נָסַךְ | Morphology: H:V

A 1) to pour out, pour, offer, cast
1a) (Qal)
1a1) to pour out
1a2) to cast metal images
1a3) to anoint (a king)
1b) (Niphal) to be anointed
1c) (Piel) to pour out (as a libation)
1d) (Hiphil) to pour out libations
1e) (Hophal) to be poured out B 1) to set, install
1a) (Qal) to install
1b) (Niphal) to be installed
