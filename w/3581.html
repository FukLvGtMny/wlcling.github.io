kōaḥ כֹּחַ ‘(a) reptile (b) strength’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: from an unused root meaning to be firm | Variants: כֹּחַ, כֹּחַ | Morphology: (a) H:N-M (b) H:N-M

(a) 1) a small reptile, probably a kind of lizard, which is unclean
1a) perhaps an extinct animal, exact meaning is unknown (b) 1) strength, power, might
1a) human strength
1b) strength (of angels)
1c) power (of God)
1d) strength (of animals)
1e) strength, produce, wealth (of soil)
