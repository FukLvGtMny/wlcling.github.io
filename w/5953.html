ʿālal עָלַל ‘1to abuse 2to glean 3to mock 4to thrust’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: a primitive root | In another language: עלל | Variants: עָלַל, עָלַל | Morphology: H:V

A 1) to act severely, deal with severely, make a fool of someone
1a) (Poel) to act severely
1b) (Poal) to be severely dealt with
1c) (Hithpael) to busy oneself, divert oneself, deal wantonly, deal ruthlessly, abuse (by thrusting through)
1d) (Hithpoel) to practise practices, thrust forth (in wickedness)
B 1) (Poel) to glean
C 1) (Poel) to act or play the child
D 1) (Poel) to insert, thrust, thrust in, thrust upon
