ʾô אוֹ ‘(a) or (b) desire’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: short for H185 | Roots: אוה | In another language: אוה (446) | Variants: אוֹ, אוֹ | Morphology: (a) H:CONJ (b) H:N-M

(a) 1) or, rather
1a) implying that the latter choice is preferred
1b) or if, introducing an example to be seen under a particular principle
1c) (in series) either...or, whether...or
1d) if perchance
1e) except, or else
2) whether, not the least, if, otherwise, also, and, then (b) 1) desire
