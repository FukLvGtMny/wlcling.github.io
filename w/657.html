ʾepes אֶ֫פֶס ‘(a) end (b) soles’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: from H656 | Roots: אפס | In another language: אפס | Variants: אֶ֫פֶס, אֹ֫פֶס | Morphology: (a) H:N-M (b) H:N-M

(a) 1) ceasing, end, finality
2) expressing non-existence (b) 1) the two extremities, i.e. either the soles of the feet or the ankles
