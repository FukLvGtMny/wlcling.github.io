hālal הָלַל ‘1to shine 2to boast 3to be foolish’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: a primitive root | Variants: הָלַל, הָלַל | Morphology: V

A 1) to shine
1a) (Qal) to shine (fig. of God's favour)
1b) (Hiphil) to flash forth light
B 1) to praise, boast, be boastful
1a) (Qal)
1a1) to be boastful
1a2) boastful ones, boasters (participle)
1b) (Piel)
1b1) to praise
1b2) to boast, make a boast
1c) (Pual)
1c1) to be praised, be made praiseworthy, be commended, be worthy of praise
1d) (Hithpael) to boast, glory, make one's boast
C 1) (Poel) to make a fool of, make into a fool
1f) (Hithpoel) to act madly, act like a madman
