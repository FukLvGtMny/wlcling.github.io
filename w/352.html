ʾayil אַ֫יִל ‘(a) ram (b) pillar (c) leader (d) terebinth’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: from the same as H193

Roots: אול

In another language: אול

Variants: אַ֫יִל, אַ֫יִל

Morphology: (a) H:N-M (b) H:N-M (c) H:N-M (d) H:N-M

(a) 1) ram
1a) ram (as food)
1b) ram (as sacrifice)
1c) ram (skin dyed red, for tabernacle) (b) 1) pillar, door post, jambs, pilaster (c) 1) strong man, leader, chief (d) 1) mighty tree, terebinth
