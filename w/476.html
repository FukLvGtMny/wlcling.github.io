ʾĕlîšāmāʿ אֱלִישָׁמָע ‘Elishama’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: from H410 and H8085 | Roots: אל שׁמע | In another language: שׁמע | Morphology: N:N-M-P

Elishama = "my God has heard"
1) an Ephraimite chief in the wilderness
2) a son of David
3) Jehoiakim's secretary
4) a priest who taught the law
5) a man of Judah
