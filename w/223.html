ʾûriyyâ אוּרִיָּה ‘(a) Uriah (b) Urijah’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: from H217 and H3050 | Roots: אור יה | Variants: אוּרִיָּ֫הוּ, אוּרִיָּה | Morphology: (a) N:N-M-P (b) N:N-M-P

(a) Uriah or Urijah = "Jehovah (Yahweh) is my light (flame)"
1) Hittite husband of Bathsheba
2) a priest who built king Ahaz' heathen altar
3) a priest who rebuilt Jerusalem's wall (b) Uriah or Urijah = "Jehovah (Yahweh) is my light (flame)"
1) a prophet slain by Jehoiakim
