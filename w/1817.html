dāl דָּל ‘(a) door (b) door (c) door’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: from H1802 | Roots: דלה | Variants: דָּל, דָּלָה, דֶּ֫לֶת | Morphology: (a) H:N-M (b) H:N-F (c) H:N-M + H:N-F

(a) 1) door (fig.) (b) 1) door (fig., door of chamber in which people (personified) hide) (c)
1) door, gate
1a) a door
1b) a gate
1c) (fig.)
1c1) of chest lid
1c2) of crocodile jaws
1c3) of doors of the heavens
1c4) of an easily-accessible woman
