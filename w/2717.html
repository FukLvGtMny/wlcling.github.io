ḥārēb חָרֵב ‘1to dry 2to destroy 3to slay’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: a primitive root | In another language: חרב | Variants: חָרֵב, חָרַב | Morphology: H:V

A 1) to be dry, be dried up
1a) (Qal) to be dried, be dried up
1b) (Pual) to be dried
1c) (Hiphil) to dry up
1d) (Hophal) to be dried up
B 1) to be waste, lay waste, make desolate, be desolate, be in ruins
1a) (Qal) to be waste, be desolate
1b) (Niphal)
1b1) to be made desolate
1b2) desolate (participle)
1c) (Hiphil) to lay waste, make desolate
1d) (Hophal) to be laid waste
C 1) to attack, smite down, slay, fight
