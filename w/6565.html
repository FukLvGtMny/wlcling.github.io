pārar פָּרַר ‘(a) to break (b) to split’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: a primitive root | In another language: פּרר | Variants: פָּרַר, פָּרַר | Morphology: (a) H:V (b) H:V

(a) 1) to break, frustrate
1a) (Hiphil)
1a1) to break, violate
1a2) to frustrate, make ineffectual
1b) (Hophal)
1b1) to be frustrated
1b2) to be broken
1b3) to break
1c) (Pilpel) to break to bits, shatter (b) 1) to split, divide
1a) (Qal) to split, crack through
1b) (Poel) to break apart
1c) (Hithpoel) to be split, be cracked through
