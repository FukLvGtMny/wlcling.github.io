ʾaḥar אַחַר ‘(a) after (b) backwards’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: from H309 | Roots: אחר | In another language: אחר | Variants: אַחַר, אַחֲרַי | Morphology: (a) H:A (b) H:ADV

(a) 1) after the following part, behind (of place), hinder, afterwards (of time)
1a) as an adverb
1a1) behind (of place)
1a2) afterwards (of time)
1b) as a preposition
1b1) behind, after (of place)
1b2) after (of time)
1b3) besides
1c) as a conjunction
1c) after that
1d) as a substantive
1d1) hinder part
1e) with other prepositions
1e1) from behind
1e2) from following after (b) 1) backwards (of a man that turns backwards)
