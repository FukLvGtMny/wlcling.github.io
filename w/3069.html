yǝhōwih יְהֹוִה ‘YHWH/God’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: a variation of H3068 (used after H136, and pronounced by Jews as H430, in order to prevent the repetition of the same sound, since they elsewhere pronounce H3068 as H136) | Roots: יהווה אדוני יהוא | Morphology: N:N-M-T

1) Jehovah-used primarily in the combination 'Lord Jehovah'
1a) equal to H03068 but pointed with the vowels of H0430
