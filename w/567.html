ʾĕmōrî אֱמֹרִי ‘Amorites’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: probably a patronymic from an unused name derived from H559 in the sense of publicity, i.e. prominence | Roots: אמר | In another language: אמר | Morphology: N:N--LG

Amorite = "a sayer"
1) one of the peoples of east Canaan and beyond the Jordan, dispossessed by the Israelite incursion from Egypt
