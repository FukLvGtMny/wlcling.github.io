śāʿir שָׂעִר ‘(a) hairy (b) he-goat (c) satyr’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: from H8175 | Roots: שׂער | In another language: שׂער | Variants: שָׂעִיר, שָׂעִר, שָׂעִיר | Morphology: (a) H:A (b) H:N-M (c) H:N-M

(a) adj
1) hairy (b) 1) he-goat, buck
1a) as sacrificial animal (c) 1) satyr, may refer to a demon possessed goat like the swine of Gadara (Mt 8:30-32)
