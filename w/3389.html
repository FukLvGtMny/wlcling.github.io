yǝrūšālaim, yǝrūšǝlēm יְרוּשָׁלִַ֫ם, יְרוּשְׁלֵם ‘Jerusalem’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: a dual (in allusion to its two main hills (the true pointing, at least of the former reading, seems to be that of H3390)); probably from (the passive participle of) H3384 and H7999 | Roots: ירושׁלם ירה ירא שׁלם | In another language: שׁלם | Morphology: N:N--L

Jerusalem = "teaching of peace"
1) the chief city of Palestine and capital of the united kingdom and the nation of Judah after the split
