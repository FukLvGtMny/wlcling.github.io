rāʿaʿ רָעַע ‘(a) be evil (b) to shatter’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: a primitive root | In another language: רעה | Variants: רָעַע, רָעַע | Morphology: (a) H:V (b) H:V

(a) 1) to be bad, be evil
1a)(Qal)
1a1) to be displeasing
1a2) to be sad
1a3) to be injurious, be evil
1a4) to be wicked, be evil (ethically)
1b) (Hiphil)
1b1) to do an injury or hurt
1b2) to do evil or wickedly
1b3) mischief (participle) (b) 1) to break, shatter
1a) (Qal)
1a1) to break
1a2) broken (participle)
1a3) to be broken
1b) (Hithpolel) to be broken, be broken in pieces, be broken asunder
