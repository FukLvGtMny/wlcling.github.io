ḥālap חָלַף ‘to pass’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: [a primitive root]

Roots:

frequency: 28, first occurence (encoded): 1031007

In another language: חלף ()

Morphology: verb

Roots:

Variants: חָלַף

Morphology: H:V

Meaning: 1) to pass on or away, pass through, pass by, go through, grow up, change, to go on from
1a) (Qal)
1a1) to pass on quickly
1a2) to pass away (vanish)
1a3) to come on anew, sprout again (of grass)
1a4) to pass through
1a5) to overstep, transgress
1b) (Piel) to cause to pass, change
1c) (Hiph)
1c1) to change, substitute, alter, change for better, renew
1c2) to show newness (of tree)
