בָּשָׂר, ‘flesh’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: [from H1319]

Roots: בשׂר

frequency: 270, first occurence (encoded): 1002021

In another language: בשׂר ()

Morphology: noun masc

Roots: ◊1319¤

Variants: בָּשָׂר

Morphology: H:N-M

Meaning: 1) flesh
1a) of the body
1a1) of humans
1a2) of animals
1b) the body itself
1c) male organ of generation (euphemism)
1d) kindred, blood-relations
1e) flesh as frail or erring (man against God)
1f) all living things
1g) animals
1h) mankind
