ʾāwen אָ֫וֶן ‘iniquity’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: from an unused root perhaps meaning properly, to pant (hence, to exert oneself, usually in vain; to come to naught) | In another language: אין | Morphology: H:N-M

1) trouble, wickedness, sorrow
1a) trouble, sorrow
1b) idolatry
1c) trouble of iniquity, wickedness
