ṣārar צָרַר ‘(a) to confine (b) to constrain (c) to distress (d) to vex (e) to rival’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: a primitive root | In another language: צרר | Variants: צָרַר, צָרַר | Morphology: (a) H:V (b) H:V (c) H:V (d) H:V (e) H:V

(a) 1) to confine, besiege (b) 1) to bind, be narrow, be in distress, make narrow, cause distress, besiege, be straitened, be bound
1a) (Qal)
1a1) to bind, tie up, shut up
1a2) to be scant, be cramped, be in straits
1b) (Pual) to be bound, be tied up
1c) (Hiphil)
1c1) to make narrow for, cause distress to, press hard upon (c) 1) to suffer distress (d) 1) to show hostility toward, vex (e) 1) to make a rival-wife
