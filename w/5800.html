ʿāzab עָזַב ‘(a) to leave (b) to restore’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: [a primitive root]

Roots:

frequency: 215, first occurence (encoded): 1002024

In another language: עזב ()

Morphology: verb

Roots:

Variants: (a) עָזַב (b) עָזַב

Morphology: (a) H:V (b) H:V

Meaning: (a) 1) to leave, loose, forsake
1a) (Qal) to leave
1a1) to depart from, leave behind, leave, let alone
1a2) to leave, abandon, forsake, neglect, apostatise
1a3) to let loose, set free, let go, free
1b) (Niphal)
1b1) to be left to
1b2) to be forsaken
1c) (Pual) to be deserted (b) 1) to restore, repair
1a) (Qal) to repair
