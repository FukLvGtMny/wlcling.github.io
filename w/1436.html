גְּדַלְיָה, ‘(a) Gedaliah (b) Gedaliah’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: [from H1431 and H3050]

Roots: גדל יה

frequency: 32, first occurence (encoded): 12025022

In another language: גדל ()

Morphology: name

Roots: ◊1431¤,◊3050¤

Variants: (a) גְּדַלְיָה (b) גְּדַלְיָ֫הוּ

Morphology: (a) N:N-M-P (b) N:N-M-P

Meaning: (a) Gedaliah = "Jehovah is great"
1) son of Ahikam, the governor of Judea appointed by Nebuchadnezzar
2) son of Amariah and grandson of Hezekiah
3) a priest of the sons of Jeshua in the time of Ezra (b) Gedaliah = "Jehovah is great"
1) a son of Jeduthun in the time of David
2) governor of Judea
3) son of Pashur, one of the chiefs of Jerusalem in the time of Jeremiah
