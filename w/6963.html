qôl קוֹל ‘(a) voice (b) frivolity’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: from an unused root meaning to call aloud | In another language: קול | Variants: קוֹל, קֹל | Morphology: (a) H:N-M (b) H:N-M

(a) 1) voice, sound, noise
1a) voice
1b) sound (of instrument) (b) 1) lightness, frivolity
