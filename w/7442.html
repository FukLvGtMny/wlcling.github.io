רוּן, ‘(a) to overcome (b) to sing’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: [a primitive root]

Roots:

frequency: 54, first occurence (encoded): 3009024

In another language: רנן ()

Morphology: verb

Roots:

Variants: (a) רוּן (b) רָנַן

Morphology: (a) H:V (b) H:V

Meaning: (a) 1) to overcome
1a) (Hithpolel) to be overcome (b) 1) to cry out, shout for joy, give a ringing cry
1a) (Qal)
1a1) to give a ringing cry (in joy, exaltation, distress)
1a2) to cry aloud (in summons, exhortation of wisdom)
1b) (Piel) to give a ringing cry (in joy, exultation, praise
1c) (Pual) ringing cry, singing out (passive)
1d) (Hiphil) to cause to ring or sing out (for joy)
1e) (Hithpolel) rejoicing (participle)
