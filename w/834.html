ʾăšer אֲשֶׁר ‘(a) which (b) in which (c) from which (d) as which’ (Hebrew)

Etymology: a primitive relative pronoun (of every gender and number)

In another language: אשׁר

Variants: (a) H:R (b) H:R (c) H:R (d) H:R

Meaning
(a) 1) (relative part.)
1a) which, who
1b) that which
2) (conj)
2a) that (in obj clause)
2b) when
2c) since
2d) as
2e) conditional if (b) 1) in (that) which
2) (adv)
2a) where
3) (conj)
3a) in that, inasmuch as
3b) on account of (c) 1) from (or than) that which
2) from (the place) where
3) from (the fact) that, since (d) conj
1) according as, as, when
1a) according to that which, according as, as
1b) with a causal force: in so far as, since
1c) with a temporal force: when
